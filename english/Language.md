# How to Write a Description Properly

Here are some tips to help make your mod more appealing to discerning users.

### Pay Attention to Formatting

Start sentences with a capital letter. Use quotation marks when necessary. Put a space after commas, colons, or semicolons. Also put spaces before and after dashes. Try to avoid double spaces between words.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> this is my mod,and what you &nbsp;going to do about it.he adds skin abracadabra to game
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> This is my mod, and what are you going to do about it? It adds the &laquo;abracadabra&raquo; skin to the game.
</div>

### Follow Language Norms

Don't forget punctuation marks. Try to write words without errors. Construct sentences properly.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> I very much to love cow therefore this mod add cow to game. Hope you like it.
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> I really **love cows,** so this mod adds **a cow** to the game. Hope you like **it**!
</div>

### Avoid Transliteration

That is, do not write English words with Russian letters. It's better to find a similar word or write it in Latin script.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> The mod adds skins from the Chinese Brawl Stars, replacing the defolt ones.
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> The mod adds skins from the Chinese version of **Brawl Stars**, replacing the **default** ones.
</div>

### Think About the Content

When writing a mod description, it's best to try to answer the questions: "why will the user find this mod useful?", "what will it change?", and "how will it affect the game?".

The more specific and the less fluff – the better. After all, nobody wants to buy a pig in a poke.

Bad:
<div style="background: #FF00001F">

> The mod adds so many skins you can't even imagine!
</div>

Good:
<div style="background: #00FF001F">

> The mod adds custom skins for **Crow, Emz, Piper, and Colette**, replacing their default skins.
</div>

### Bonus: A Small Tip

If you struggle with language and formatting rules, you can ask an LLM (e.g., ChatGPT, DeepSeek) for help. This can yield acceptable results in most cases. But don't overdo it — generated texts may feel off-putting even if they are linguistically correct.
